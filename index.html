Quick Guide

Everything You
Need to Know

Answers to the most common questions about visiting Salt Lake City.

🌤️ What's the weather like?
SLC has four distinct seasons. Summers are warm and dry (70–95°F), perfect for hiking. Winters are cold with heavy mountain snowfall (20–35°F in the valley). Spring and fall are mild and beautiful. The valley typically gets less snow than the nearby canyons.
🚗 How do I get around?
TRAX light rail connects downtown to the airport and suburbs. UTA buses cover the metro area. Many neighborhoods are walkable. Uber/Lyft are readily available. For ski canyons, renting a car is strongly recommended as public transit to resorts is limited.
🍺 What's the alcohol situation?
Utah has unique liquor laws. Bars and restaurants serve full-strength alcohol but there are some quirks — grocery stores sell beer limited to 5% ABV. State Liquor Stores (DABC) sell wine and spirits. The craft beer scene is thriving despite the laws.
💰 Is SLC expensive?
SLC has grown significantly in cost but remains more affordable than Denver, Seattle, or San Francisco. Average rent is $1,300–$1,800/month. Meals range from $10 budget spots to $50+ fine dining. Ski lift tickets are $100–$180/day. See our Plan page for a full budget guide.
🏳️‍🌈 Is SLC LGBTQ+ friendly?
Yes! SLC has a vibrant and growing LGBTQ+ community centered around the 9th & 9th neighborhood and the Capitol Hill area. Utah Pride Festival (June) is one of the region's largest. The city is notably more progressive than the surrounding state.

📍 Getting There

✈️ By Air: Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) — recently renovated, direct flights from 90+ cities. TRAX light rail connects airport to downtown in 25 min.
🚗 By Car: I-15 is the main artery. 4.5 hrs from Las Vegas, 8 hrs from Denver, 6.5 hrs from Phoenix.
🚆 By Train: Amtrak's California Zephyr stops at SLC, connecting to San Francisco (17 hrs) and Denver (11 hrs).
