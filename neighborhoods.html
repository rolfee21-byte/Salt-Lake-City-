Sugar House

SLC's coolest neighborhood — indie shops & cafés
Trendy LGBTQ+ Friendly Foodie Young

Sugar House is consistently voted SLC's most popular neighborhood — a dense, walkable enclave packed with independent coffee shops, restaurants, boutiques, and bars. The Sugar House Business District along 2100 S is the city's best commercial strip for local businesses. Beloved Sugar House Park offers a stunning mountain backdrop.

The Avenues

Historic Victorian streets with mountain trailheads
Historic Walkable Trails Family

The Avenues is one of SLC's most desirable residential neighborhoods — a hillside grid of Victorian homes, tree-lined streets, and incredible Wasatch views. Streets run in a named grid (A through V) climbing the foothills. Direct access to the Bonneville Shoreline Trail makes it paradise for hikers and runners.

Capitol Hill

Historic hilltop district with sweeping valley views
Historic Views Quiet

Perched above downtown on the north side, Capitol Hill surrounds the stunning Utah State Capitol building. The neighborhood features a mix of Victorian mansions, converted row houses, and excellent views of the entire valley. The Marmalade District — its charming eastern section — has some of SLC's oldest homes and streets named after fruits.

🏛️
Utah State Capitol — free tours and panoramic views
🏡

Granary District

SLC's creative arts district — galleries & studios
Arts District Industrial Breweries Studios

SLC's rapidly evolving arts district — a converted industrial neighborhood just south of downtown packed with galleries, artist studios, craft breweries, and independent music venues. The Utah Museum of Contemporary Art's Future Home is anchoring a major arts corridor here. Think Brooklyn before the rents skyrocketed.

University / East Bench

College energy meets mountain trailheads
Academic Young Trails Arts

The University of Utah's stunning campus sits against the Wasatch foothills, making this one of the most scenic urban university settings in the US. The surrounding neighborhoods are lively, diverse, and full of excellent cafés, museums, and direct trail access. Red Butte Garden and Natural History Museum anchor the upper end.

Rose Park

Diverse, affordable, and full of character
Diverse Affordable Community Cultural

Rose Park is one of SLC's most culturally diverse and rapidly improving neighborhoods — home to a thriving Latino community, excellent authentic food options, and strong neighborhood pride. Jordan River access and proximity to the airport make it convenient. A great area for authentic local experiences off the tourist trail.

Central Ninth

SLC's fastest-growing hipster hub
Trendy Fast-Growing Coffee Bars

Central Ninth is Salt Lake City's fastest-evolving neighborhood — a compact strip of 900 S between Main and 300 W that's packed with boutique coffee shops, natural wine bars, craft cocktail lounges, and independent restaurants. Think Portland vibes in the desert. Increasingly popular with young creatives and remote workers.
