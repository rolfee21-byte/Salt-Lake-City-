Arrivals

Getting to Salt Lake City

✈️ By Air

Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) was recently completely rebuilt — it's now one of the most beautiful and modern airports in the US. Direct flights from 90+ cities across the US, Canada, and Europe.

LAX → SLC~1.5 hrs
ORD → SLC~2.5 hrs
JFK → SLC~4.5 hrs
LHR → SLC~10 hrs
🚊 TRAX light rail connects airport to downtown in 25 minutes for $2.50

🚗 By Car

I-15 is the main artery running north-south through the Wasatch Front. SLC is well-connected to major western cities by road.

🌴 Las Vegas4.5 hrs
🌵 Phoenix6.5 hrs
🏔️ Denver8 hrs
🌲 Portland11 hrs

🚆 By Train

Amtrak's California Zephyr stops at Salt Lake City Amtrak Station, connecting to major cities via scenic mountain routes.

California Zephyr
Chicago – Denver – SLC – San Francisco
SF → SLC: ~17 hrs
Denver → SLC: ~11 hrs
🌿 Most scenic train ride in North America — crossing the Rockies!
Transport

Getting Around SLC

🚊

TRAX Light Rail

3 lines connecting downtown to the airport, University of Utah, Sandy (RSL stadium), and the suburbs. Runs daily 5am–midnight. Fare: $2.50. Free in the downtown "Free Fare Zone" (main streets). Best for: airport, U of U, major attractions.

🚌

UTA Bus System

Salt Lake City's bus network covers the metro area comprehensively. Multiple routes connect to suburbs, sugar house, and major destinations. Same fare as TRAX ($2.50, transfers included).

🚲

Green Bike Share

500+ bikes and e-bikes at stations across the city. $2/trip or $30/month. SLC's flat downtown grid is perfect for cycling. Perfect for short hops between neighborhoods.

🚗

Uber / Lyft

Widely available throughout the metro. Essential for ski canyon trips (no transit to Alta, Snowbird, Brighton, or Solitude). Surge pricing on ski days — book early or use the ski resort shuttles.

🎿 Getting to Ski Resorts

No transit goes directly to Snowbird, Alta, or Brighton. Your options:

🚌 Canyon Transportation
Shared shuttle from SLC hotels to resorts. ~$30–45 roundtrip. Book 24 hrs ahead.
🚗 Rental Car
Best flexibility. Rentals from airport from $40/day. 4WD recommended in winter — but not required.
⚡ Rideshare
Uber/Lyft to ski canyons runs $45–80 each way. Gets expensive fast — share with others.
🚌 UTA to Park City
Bus route 902 connects to Park City Mountain and Deer Valley from downtown SLC. $5 each way!
Accommodation

Where to Stay

SLC has accommodation for every budget — from boutique hotels to budget hostels.

🏙️

Downtown SLC

Best for: city exploration, Temple Square, concerts, dining. Hilton, Marriott, Grand America, and boutique hotels. TRAX airport connection. Walkable to major attractions.

$$$: $150–$350/night
🍬

Sugar House / 9th & 9th

Best for: local experience, indie restaurants, neighborhood vibe. B&Bs, vacation rentals, and boutique options. 3 miles from downtown, accessible by TRAX.

$$: $100–$200/night
🎿

Little Cottonwood / Big Cottonwood Base Areas

Best for: skiers wanting slope access. Cliff Lodge (Snowbird), Alta Ski Lodges, Solitude Mountain Resort. Slope-side convenience. Car not required for skiing.

$$$$: $250–$700/night
💎

Park City

Best for: ski resort access + vibrant town. 35 min from SLC but a completely different experience — charming historic Main Street, world-class dining, and close to Park City Mountain and Deer Valley.

$$$$: $300–$800/night
💰

Murray / Sandy / Midvale (Budget)

Best for: budget travelers. Suburban areas south of SLC with chain hotels at great prices. TRAX-connected to downtown. 20–35 minutes to the city center or ski canyons.

$: $70–$150/night

💡 Insider Tips
  • Book ski days midweek — weekends at Snowbird/Alta can be brutal
  • Temple Square is free and always worth a visit
  • Red Iguana always has a line — go at 5pm or wait 45+ min
  • TRAX is free in the downtown core
  • Check AirNow.gov before visiting — valley inversion can cause bad air quality in winter
