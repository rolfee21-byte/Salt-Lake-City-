Natural History Museum of Utah

🦕
★★★★★4.8 · 6,200+ reviews

The Natural History Museum of Utah is one of North America's finest natural history museums. Located in the stunning Rio Tinto Center — an award-winning building that rises from the foothills like the rocky landscape it celebrates — the museum features world-class dinosaur fossil collections, including some of Utah's own species discovered in the Morrison Formation.

Beyond dinosaurs, explore Utah's deep geological history, ancient Indigenous cultures spanning 12,000 years, and the remarkable Great Salt Lake ecosystem.

Address
301 Wakara Way, Salt Lake City
Hours
Daily 9:30am – 5:30pm
Admission
Adults $21 · Kids $14
Time Needed
2–4 hours
Visit Website

Liberty Park

🌳
★★★★★4.7 · 5,300+ reviews

Liberty Park is Salt Lake City's crown jewel urban park — 80 acres of lush green space in the heart of the city. With a 6-acre pond, a children's amusement area, tennis courts, a skate park, and an outdoor swimming pool, it's the city's ultimate gathering place.

On weekends it buzzes with activity: families picnicking, musicians jamming, cyclists circling the perimeter path, and residents of every background enjoying this democratic outdoor space.

Address
600 E 900 S, Salt Lake City
Hours
Open Daily 6am – 10pm
Admission
Free
Best For
Families, picnics, sports

Temple Square

⛪
★★★★★4.6 · 12,000+ reviews

Temple Square is the centerpiece of downtown Salt Lake City and the global headquarters of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The iconic Salt Lake Temple — 40 years in the making and completed in 1893 — dominates the skyline with its six granite spires reaching 210 feet into the sky.

The square also contains the historic Tabernacle (home to the famous Mormon Tabernacle Choir), Assembly Hall, the Family History Library, and beautifully manicured gardens. The Christmas lights display is world-famous, drawing hundreds of thousands of visitors each December.

Address
50 N Temple, Salt Lake City
Hours
Daily 9am – 10pm (grounds)
Admission
Free
Don't Miss

Great Salt Lake

🌊

The Great Salt Lake is the largest saltwater lake in the Western Hemisphere and one of Utah's most extraordinary natural wonders. Its high salinity turns parts of the lake a striking pink color from salt-loving algae. The lake is a critical stopover for millions of migratory birds.

Best For
