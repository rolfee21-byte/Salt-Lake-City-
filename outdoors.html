Trail Tips

🌡️ Canyon temps are 10–15°F cooler than the valley — always bring a layer
💧 Carry at least 2L of water per person for hikes over 5 miles
🐻 Wildlife is real — carry bear spray on high alpine trails
🅿️ Many popular trailheads fill by 8am on weekends — go early!
📱 Download offline maps (Gaia GPS or AllTrails) — cell signal is spotty
🌩️ Watch for afternoon thunderstorms July–September above treeline

Cycling in SLC

From paved urban greenways to gnarly mountain bike singletracks, SLC has world-class riding for every skill level.

🚵
Mountain Biking

Millcreek MTB Trails

25+ miles of singletrack from beginner-friendly rollers to advanced techy lines. The Pipeline Trail is a local classic.

All Levels · Free access
🚲
Road Cycling

Big & Little Cottonwood Canyons

Two iconic cycling climbs starting from the valley floor. Big Cottonwood is 15 miles to Brighton (3,000 ft gain), Little Cottonwood to Snowbird is 9 miles.

Intermediate–Hard
🛴
Urban Cycling

Jordan River Parkway

A 40-mile paved multi-use trail running the full length of the Jordan River — perfect for leisurely rides, skating, and walking through SLC's communities.

Easy · Free · Flat
⚡
E-Bike Rental

Green Bike SLC

SLC's bike-share program with 500+ bikes and e-bikes at stations across the city. Perfect for flat urban exploring — monthly or daily memberships available.

From $2/ride or $30/month
🏁
Race

Tour of Utah & Crusher in Tushar

SLC hosts elite cycling races annually. The Crusher in Tushar near Beaver is a beloved gravel race through dramatic high-altitude terrain.

Annual events · Check calendar
🌄
Mountain Biking

Bonneville Shoreline Trail

An 80+ mile trail system tracing the ancient shoreline of Lake Bonneville along the entire Wasatch Front — epic views of the valley throughout.

Intermediate · Free
Vertical SLC

Rock Climbing & Bouldering

SLC is a climbing hub with world-class crags 20 minutes from downtown and some of the best climbing gyms in the country.

🧗 Outdoor Crags

Little Cottonwood Canyon
All Grades

World-class granite trad and sport climbing — 700+ routes from 5.5 to 5.14+. Tombstone Buttress and Suicide Wall are iconic. 28 mi from SLC.

Big Cottonwood Canyon
Intermediate

Sport climbing focus with fantastic moderate routes. Storm Mountain Picnic Area is the main access point with over 200 routes.

City of Rocks (Burley, ID)
All Levels

A 3-hour drive but absolutely worth it — 600+ routes on dramatic granite spires in a stunning semi-arid landscape. A bucket-list climbing destination.

Mill Creek Bouldering
Beginner–V8

Bouldering close to the city in Millcreek Canyon. Great warm-up area and accessible year-round when snowfree. Popular with locals.

🏋️ Climbing Gyms

The Front Climbing Club
Top Rated

SLC's premier climbing gym — massive facility with top-rope, lead, and bouldering. Two locations (SLC & Ogden). Excellent training area.

1470 S 400 W, SLC
Day pass ~$22

Three SLC-area gyms (Millcreek, Murray, Sandy) — huge bouldering caves, dedicated youth programs, and excellent route-setting.

Multiple Locations

744 E Winchester St, Murray

Water & Lakes

Water Recreation

🏊

Float the Great Salt Lake

You literally can't sink — the high salinity makes floating effortless. Access at Antelope Island, Saltair, or Great Salt Lake State Park.

Learn More

Jordanelle & Deer Creek

Reservoirs 40 minutes from SLC offering kayaking, paddleboarding, wake boarding, and fishing in a gorgeous mountain setting.

Open May–Oct

The Provo River and Green River (2.5 hrs) are legendary trout fisheries. The Provo has a Gold Medal catch-and-release section right outside SLC.

License Required
